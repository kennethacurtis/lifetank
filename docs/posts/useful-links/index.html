Useful Links for Anyone That Uses the Internet

It’s the day after Thanksgiving, you’re sitting around and wondering “why have I looked at the same links on Reddit five times already?”. Or maybe you just need to look busy to avoid contact with those other humans in your house. You’re in luck, because here is a collection of some interesting sites that may help you be (or look) productive. I will start with my favorites, then list some sites according to category

Favorites

archive.org - The Internet Archive

This is your one stop shop for so many different resources, and it’s all free. Music, movies, books, software, you name it. You can even play a large collection of classic PC games. The Internet Archive also includes The Wayback Machine which lets you explore more than 486 billion web pages saved over time. Here’s what Myspace looked like on June 7th, 2005.

furturetimeline.net

From the site:

London-based writer and futurist, William James Fox, started Future Timeline back in 2008. It began as a relatively small and obscure website with a brief list of future predictions. Over the years, however, it expanded to form a lengthy and detailed timeline – running from the present day, through the next century and beyond, all the way to the end of the universe itself.

For example, I browsed over to the 2090 predictions. This is the decade I will be turning 80 years old. During this decade, it’s said that:

  • Religion is fading from European culture
  • Hypersonic vactrains are widespread
  • SpaceX’s “Starman” has a close encounter with Earth
  • West Antarctica is among the fastest developing regions in the world
  • Hurricane defence fields are operational in the U.S.
  • Global fertility has stabilised at below 2.0 children per woman
  • Many of the world’s languages are no longer in use
  • Sea levels are wreaking havoc around the world
  • Over 80% of the Amazon rainforest has been lost
  • The average employee works less than 20 hours per week

Just that section alone provided some really interesting content on what the future may hold.

futureme.org

Ever wanted to hear from yourself in the past? Write an email and get it sent to you in the future. This could turn in a very self reflective process.

ninite.com

Install all of your programs at once, without any stop gates, questions, or toolbars. Here is a full list of all the applications it supports. For free you can select all of the applications you want and download a customized installer/updater. There’s also a paid pro version with great features for companies that have many machines to manage and patch.

10minutemail.com

This site is useful if you need a quick email to read or sign up for something you don’t really care about. This has dwindled down my spam by a good amount.

pointerpointer.com

This is a completey useless site. But one thing is for sure, it will always point towards your pointer.

Other favorites

Utility

  • deepl.com - An AI translator with much more natural responses.
  • tip-of-my-tongue - Find that word that you’ve been thinking about all day but just can’t seem to remember.
  • Runpee.com - We’ve all been there, enjoying a movie, when nature calls. It starts with a whisper, then becomes a shout. Before you know it, you’re not watching the movie so much as watching for a chance to make a mad dash to the restroom.

Consumers

  • camelcamelcamel.com - Free Amazon price tracker monitors millions of products and alerts you when prices drop, helping you decide when to buy.
  • wornontv.net - a site dedicated to finding the exact same clothes that actors wear on your favorite movies or TV shows.
  • justwatch.com - Search any movie or TV show and see where it’s streaming, the app also gives you recommendations.
  • tela party (browser extension) - lets you easily watch movies in sync with friends.

Productivity

  • photopea.com - online photo editor with photoshop level features.
  • remove.bg - remove a background front a subject (this is amazing btw, it does lower image quality a little).
  • unsplash.com - Tons of free-to-use images of damn near everything.
  • pexels.com - Same as unsplash but more off beat stuff.
  • thenounproject.com - free to use icons of everything.
  • radio.garden - listen to radio from around the world.
  • imslp.org - A library of public domain classical sheet music. You can pretty much find any classical music over 100 years old.
  • toffeeshare.com - lets you share files privately, without size limit, with end-to-end encryption and without them being stored online.

Learning

  • z-lib.org - search for a PDF of any text book on any subject you can find for free.
  • lightningmaps.org - A real time map of lightning strikes.
  • windy.com - very robust weather map with multiple layers of information you can turn on and off.
  • howmanypeopleareinspacerightnow.com - see title, as of today there are 7
  • wolfamalpha.com - “Compute expert-level answers using Wolfram’s breakthrough algorithms, knowledgebase and AI technology”
  • desmos.com - If you need a graphing calculator.
  • FlightRadar24.com - Tracking of airplane movements over the world.
  • stellarium-web.org - track planets and starts, perfect for astronomers.
  • erowid.org - Documents the relationship between humans and psychoactives. An educational site with information about recreational drugs and plants, and even documents experiences with drugs.

Happy Holidays Everyone.
